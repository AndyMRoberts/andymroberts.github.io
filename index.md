# Welcome

Hello, my name is Andy Roberts, I'm a Computing Science PhD Student at Newcastle University. I'm Studying how to leverage AI for use on agricultural robots, with the aim to increase widespread use of agri-robots in order to decrease pesticides, herbicides and fertiliser overuse. Through advancing agricultural robotic technologies we can make farming more effective and friendlier for the environment. My particular focus is on AI approaches for visual-based sensors, power optimisation, and edge computing.

Before this PhD I spent 10 years growing as a multi-skilled engineer with a first class Physics Masters and professional experience across a wide range of areas including programming, mechanical and electronic design, product manufacturing, servicing, technical support and managing a small team. I have a particular interest in
automation; incorporating expertise in Python, C++, ROS, Labview/Teststand and SQL database design.

I love puzzles, logical problems and thinking creatively, which is why I've found myself suited so well to a career in a highly applied field of computer science research. Its allows me to explore my natural curiosity over a wide range of topics and hopefully help humanity as a whole.
